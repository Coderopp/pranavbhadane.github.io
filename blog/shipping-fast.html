Ship Fast, Learn Faster

Why Velocity Matters

In early-stage product development, the speed at which you can iterate and learn from user feedback often matters more than building the perfect solution from day one. Here's why:

  • Market validation happens faster ‚Äî You discover what users actually want vs. what you think they want
  • You build momentum ‚Äî Shipping creates energy and attracts early adopters
  • You reduce waste ‚Äî Less time spent building features nobody needs

The Iteration Loop

The most successful products I've built followed this pattern:

  1. Ship a minimal version
  2. Gather user feedback aggressively
  3. Identify the biggest pain point
  4. Fix it and ship again
  5. Repeat
"Perfect is the enemy of shipped." ‚Äî Someone smarter than me

Practical Tips

Here's what this looks like in practice:

  • Set weekly or bi-weekly shipping goals
  • Use feature flags to test in production
  • Talk to users within 24 hours of shipping
  • Measure what matters, ignore vanity metrics

When to Slow Down

There are times when you should pump the brakes: security issues, scaling problems, or when technical debt is blocking velocity. But those should be the exception, not the rule.

The default should always be: ship it.
